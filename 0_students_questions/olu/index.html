The Housing Market - At A Glance

Housing prices have been soaring globally, especially over the past decade. While certain demographics benefit from this, the majority of people face the daunting ramifications of a heated property market. Below is exploration of how housing prices have changed in recent times. Specifically, the global and Australian housing market will be briefly inspected before more detailed insight is provided on Melbourne's market.


Indices Rapidly Rose in the 2010's

Real Prices: Nominal house price index to the consumer's expenditure deflator that compares pricing between each country.

Rent Prices: Changes in prices that households pay for housing rentals.

Price to Income: A measure of affordability that divides the nominal house price index by the nominal disposable income per person.

Price to Rent: A measure of profitability of house ownership that divdes the nominal house price index housing rent price index.

Using "Real Prices", it is apparent that nations like China, Canada and the US currently have a overheated housing market. Meanwhile, Australia's property market has remained relatively stable in comparison to others.

Note: Missing data is due to a lack of collection tools, possibly signifying a weak national economy.


The Australia Housing Market is Booming

Meanwhile, mainland Australia's east cost seems to have the highest overall pricing, possibly due to population. Overall, increasing trend.
